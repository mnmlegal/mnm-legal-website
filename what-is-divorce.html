What Is Divorce In India? Complete Legal Guide by MNM Legal Associates

Marriage is an important legal and social institution in India. However, when differences between spouses become irreconcilable and it becomes impossible to continue the marital relationship, the law provides a legal remedy through divorce. Divorce is the legal dissolution of a marriage by a competent court. Once a decree of divorce is granted, the marital relationship between the husband and wife legally comes to an end. Divorce proceedings in India are governed by various personal laws depending upon the religion of the parties. Types of Divorce in India 1. Mutual Consent Divorce A Mutual Consent Divorce is filed when both spouses mutually agree to dissolve their marriage. The parties voluntarily decide to separate and settle important issues such as maintenance, alimony, child custody, visitation rights and financial responsibilities. Mutual Consent Divorce is generally considered less stressful and less time-consuming than contested divorce proceedings. 2. Contested Divorce A Contested Divorce is filed when one spouse seeks divorce but the other spouse does not agree. In such cases, the matter is decided by the Family Court based on the facts, evidence and applicable law. Contested Divorce may involve issues relating to cruelty, desertion, adultery, mental illness or other legally recognized grounds under applicable personal laws. Child Custody and Maintenance During divorce proceedings, the Court may also decide matters relating to child custody, visitation rights, maintenance and alimony. The welfare of the child remains the primary consideration in custody matters. Maintenance may be claimed by an eligible spouse and dependent children depending upon the facts and circumstances of the case. Courts generally consider income, financial status, responsibilities and other relevant factors while deciding maintenance applications. Documents Commonly Required for Divorce The documents required may vary from case to case. However, commonly required documents include: • Identity Proof • Address Proof • Marriage Certificate (if available) • Marriage Photographs • Income Documents • Details relating to children (if applicable) • Other supporting documents relevant to the case Why Legal Guidance is Important Every divorce matter involves unique facts and legal considerations.
