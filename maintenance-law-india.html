Maintenance in India: Complete Legal Guide by MNM Legal Associates

Maintenance is one of the most important legal rights available under Indian law. The purpose of maintenance is to ensure that a spouse, child or dependent parent who is unable to maintain themselves receives financial support from the person legally responsible for providing such support.

Many people believe that maintenance is available only after divorce. However, maintenance may be claimed in several situations depending upon the facts of the case and the applicable law.

What is Maintenance?

Maintenance refers to financial support provided by one person to another person who is legally entitled to receive such support. The objective is to prevent financial hardship and ensure that the dependent person can maintain a reasonable standard of living.

Maintenance may be claimed by:

  • Wife
  • Children
  • Dependent Parents
  • In certain circumstances, Husband (subject to applicable law)

The amount of maintenance depends on various factors including income, financial status, liabilities, lifestyle, health condition and responsibilities of the parties.

Types of Maintenance in India

1. Interim Maintenance

Interim Maintenance is temporary financial assistance granted during the pendency of court proceedings. The purpose is to ensure that the applicant can meet day-to-day expenses while the case is pending before the Court.

2. Permanent Maintenance

Permanent Maintenance may be awarded after the final disposal of a case. Depending upon the facts and circumstances, the Court may direct payment of a lump sum amount or periodic payments.

Who Can Claim Maintenance?

Wife

A wife who is unable to maintain herself may seek maintenance from her husband under applicable legal provisions.

Children

Minor children are generally entitled to financial support from their parents. Courts may determine maintenance based on the child's educational, medical and living expenses.

Parents

Dependent parents may also claim maintenance from their children under certain legal provisions if they are unable to maintain themselves.

Factors Considered by the Court

While deciding maintenance applications, Courts generally consider:

  • Income of both parties
  • Standard of living
  • Financial obligations
  • Educational expenses of children
  • Medical expenses
  • Assets and liabilities
  • Employment status
  • Overall circumstances of the parties

There is no fixed formula for determining maintenance. Every case is decided on its own facts and merits.

Documents Commonly Required

  • Identity Proof
  • Address Proof
  • Marriage Documents
  • Income Proof
  • Salary Slips
  • Bank Statements
  • Income Tax Returns
  • Children's Educational Records
  • Medical Documents (if applicable)
  • Other supporting evidence

Maintenance and Divorce

Maintenance issues frequently arise during divorce proceedings. Courts may decide matters relating to Interim Maintenance, Permanent Alimony, Child Support, Educational Expenses, Medical Expenses, Residence and Living Costs.

The Court examines the financial circumstances of both parties before passing appropriate orders.

Maintenance in Domestic Violence Cases

Maintenance may also be claimed in matters involving domestic violence depending upon the facts and applicable legal provisions. Courts may pass appropriate orders to protect the financial interests of the aggrieved person.

Why Legal Guidance is Important?

Every maintenance matter is unique and professional legal advice can assist in presenting relevant facts and evidence before the Court.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Can maintenance be claimed without divorce?

Yes. Depending upon the circumstances and applicable law, maintenance may be claimed even without obtaining a divorce.

How is maintenance calculated?

There is no fixed formula. Courts consider income, expenses, lifestyle, liabilities and other relevant factors before determining maintenance.

Can maintenance be modified later?

Depending upon changes in circumstances, parties may seek modification of maintenance orders before the competent Court.

Can child maintenance be claimed separately?

Yes. Courts may grant maintenance for minor children based on their educational, medical and daily living requirements.

Is legal representation necessary?

Professional legal guidance helps parties understand legal procedures, documentation requirements and available remedies.
